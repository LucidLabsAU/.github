## PART 2: CUSTOMER CLAIMING RULES

### Rule 1: One Customer = One Project (Per Engagement Type)

**For Azure Accelerate Partner-Nominated Engagements:**

| Engagement Type | Can You Claim Same Customer Multiple Times? |
|-----------------|---------------------------------------------|
| **Core Migrate & Modernize (SMB)** | ✅ YES - Different workloads, different claims |
| **Data Platform (SMB)** | ✅ YES - Different analytics projects, different claims |
| **AI Apps & Developer (SMB)** | ✅ YES - Different AI/app projects, different claims |

**Important Rules:**
- ✅ **Same customer, different engagement types** = ALLOWED
  - Example: Customer A for Core Migrate ($12K) + Customer A for Data Platform ($12K) = $24K total
  
- ✅ **Same customer, different workloads, same engagement type** = ALLOWED (sequential claims)
  - Example: Customer A migration project 1 (complete) → Customer A migration project 2 (new claim)
  
- ❌ **Same customer, same engagement type, concurrent** = NOT ALLOWED
  - Can only have ONE active claim per customer per engagement type at a time

## PART 4: THE 5-STAGE CLAIMING PROCESS

### Overview of Stages:

| Stage | Name | What Happens | Timeline | Partner Action |
|-------|------|--------------|----------|----------------|
| **1** | **Nominate Customer** | Partner adds customer to engagement | 30 days max | Click "Add Customer" in Partner Center |
| **2** | **Customer Consent** | Customer approves engagement | Auto from Stage 1 | Request consent in Partner Center |
| **3** | **Execution & POE** | Partner delivers project | 60-260 days* | Complete work, gather proof |
| **4** | **POE Validation** | Microsoft reviews submission | 30 days max | Respond if more info needed |
| **5** | **Payment** | Microsoft pays partner | 45 days max | Receive payment |

*Timeline depends on project size (XXS=120 days, L=260 days)

---

### STAGE 1: Nominate Customer (30 Days Max)

**What You Do:**
1. Confirm customer intent and alignment to do the engagement
2. Log into Partner Center → Incentives → MCI Engagements
3. Click "Add Customer"
4. Enter customer details:
   - Customer name
   - TPID (if you have it)
   - Planned Azure consumption
   - Engagement type
   - Project size tier

**System Validation:**
- Partner Center validates customer eligibility
- Checks if customer has valid TPID
- Verifies customer not already claimed by another partner for same engagement

**Timeline:** You have 30 days to complete nomination after identifying customer

**Important:**
- Customer must have confirmed intent before you nominate
- Don't nominate until customer is committed
- System prevents duplicate nominations

---

### STAGE 2: Customer Consent (Within 30 Days of Nomination)

**What You Do:**
1. Partner Center sends consent request to customer
2. You follow up with customer to ensure they respond
3. Customer receives email with consent form
4. Customer clicks link and provides consent

**What Happens:**
- ✅ **If customer consents:** Moves to Stage 3 (Execution)
- ❌ **If customer doesn't consent within 30 days:** Claim automatically expires
- ⚠️ **If customer declines:** Claim is cancelled

**Best Practice:**
- Prepare customer in advance that consent request is coming
- Have customer expect the email
- Follow up immediately when consent is sent
- Typical consent rate: 90%+ if customer is properly prepared

**Pro Tip:** 
Get verbal agreement from customer BEFORE nominating. Say: "We'll send you a consent form through Microsoft's Partner Center - please approve it within 30 days so we can proceed."

---

### STAGE 3: Execution & POE Submission (60-260 Days)

**Timeline by Project Size:**

| Size | Execution Timeline | Total Time from Consent |
|------|-------------------|-------------------------|
| XXS | 120 days | 120 days |
| XS | 120 days | 120 days |
| S | 120 days | 120 days |
| M | 200 days | 200 days |
| L | 260 days | 260 days |

**What You Do:**

#### A. Deliver the Engagement
Execute the project according to engagement requirements:

**For Core Migrate & Modernize:**
1. Infrastructure/Application Compatibility Assessment/Review
2. Landing Zone Setup or Review
3. Deployment of Migration/Modernization activities
4. Secure the Azure workloads

**For Data Platform:**
1. Landing zone setup or review
2. Deployment of Microsoft Fabric and/or Azure Databricks services
3. Migration of data warehouse platforms OR
4. Implementation of new analytics use cases

**For AI Apps & Developer:**
1. Landing zone setup or review
2. Deployment of AI/Developer workloads (Azure OpenAI, AKS, App Service, etc.)

#### B. Gather Proof of Execution (POE)

**Required Documentation:**

1. **Customer Survey** (triggered by partner, completed by customer)
   - Customer validates work was completed
   - Customer confirms satisfaction
   - Automated through Partner Center

2. **Partner Survey** (completed by partner)
   - Describe work performed
   - Confirm milestones achieved
   - Document technical approach

3. **Technical Proof of Execution Documents:**
   - Architecture diagrams
   - Migration assessment reports
   - Deployment screenshots
   - Azure Monitor/usage data showing workloads deployed
   - Landing zone configuration evidence
   - Security configuration screenshots
   - **For Data Platform:** Fabric/Databricks workspace screenshots, data pipeline evidence
   - **For AI Apps:** AI service deployments, app screenshots, GitHub repo links

**Pro Tips:**
- Document everything as you go (don't wait until end)
- Take screenshots during deployment
- Save all assessment reports
- Capture before/after metrics
- Get customer quotes/testimonials

#### C. Submit Claim in Partner Center

**When to Submit:**
- Project must be complete (all milestones delivered)
- Customer survey must be completed
- All POE documentation ready
- Within the timeline for your project size

**How to Submit:**
1. Navigate to Partner Center → Incentives → MCI Engagements
2. Find your customer in the engagement
3. Under Action column, click "View engagement claim"
4. Complete all required POE elements:
   - Upload technical documentation
   - Complete partner survey
   - Confirm customer survey completed
5. Click "Submit"

**⚠️ Important Deadlines:**
- You have **90 days from customer consent** to submit claim
- If you miss deadline, claim automatically expires
- Expired claims cannot be recovered

---

### STAGE 4: POE Validation (30 Days Max)

**What Microsoft Does:**
- Reviews all POE documentation
- Validates customer survey responses
- Checks technical evidence matches engagement requirements
- Verifies Azure consumption (for some engagement types)

**What Happens:**

**If POE is Complete:**
- Microsoft approves claim
- Moves to Stage 5 (Payment)
- You receive approval notification

**If POE Needs More Info:**
- Microsoft reaches out to partner
- Partner has opportunity to provide additional documentation
- Resubmit within specified timeframe (usually 10-15 days)

**If POE is Rejected:**
- Microsoft declines claim
- Partner receives explanation of why
- Opportunity to appeal or correct and resubmit (depending on reason)

**Common Rejection Reasons:**
- Insufficient technical documentation
- Customer survey not completed
- Work doesn't meet engagement requirements
- Planned Azure consumption doesn't match tier claimed
- Missing required milestones

**Approval Rate:** Typically 85-95% if documentation is thorough

---

### STAGE 5: Payment (45 Days Max from Approval)

**What Happens:**
- Microsoft processes approved claim
- Payment issued within 45 days from month-end of approval
- Payment deposited to bank account on file

**Payment Timing Examples:**
- Claim approved January 15 → Payment by mid-March
- Claim approved January 31 → Payment by mid-March
- Claim approved February 1 → Payment by mid-April

**Payment Breakdown:**
- **Rebate portion** (typically 60-100%): Paid as indirect rebate
- **Co-op portion** (typically 0-40%): May have usage requirements

**Tax Documentation:**
- Form 1099 issued (US partners)
- Tax documentation varies by country

## PART 7: CSP MOTION INCENTIVE - ONGOING REVENUE

### How CSP Motion Works (Different from Project-Based)

**Key Difference:**
- Project-based = ONE-TIME payment per project
- CSP Motion = MONTHLY recurring payments based on consumption

### CSP Motion Incentive Structure:

**You earn monthly on ALL Azure consumption for customers you're the CSP transacting partner for**

| Lever | Rate | Monthly Payout Example |
|-------|------|----------------------|
| **Pay-as-you-go consumption** | 3.00% | Customer spends $10K/mo → You earn $300/mo |
| **Reservations & Savings Plans** | 3.00% | Customer commits $5K/mo → You earn $150/mo |
| **Growth Accelerator** | 7.50% | Customer grows $3K/mo → You earn $225/mo |

**Example Customer:**
- Base consumption: $10,000/month × 3% = **$300/month**
- Growth acceleration: $3,000/month × 7.5% = **$225/month**
- **Total: $525/month = $6,300/year from ONE customer**

## PART 9: COMMON QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

### Q: Can I claim the same customer for multiple engagement types at once?
**A:** YES! You can have concurrent claims for different engagement types. Example: Same customer for Core Migrate + Data Platform + AI Apps = 3 separate payouts.

### Q: Can I re-nominate a customer after completing a project?
**A:** YES! After completing and getting paid for one project, you can nominate the same customer for a new project (different workload).

### Q: What if customer's actual Azure spend is less than planned?
**A:** You still get paid based on **planned** consumption at time of claim, not actual. However, Microsoft may audit and could claw back if actual is significantly lower. Be realistic in estimates.

### Q: What if customer's actual Azure spend is MORE than planned?
**A:** You don't get extra for the project-based fee, but you DO benefit if you're the CSP partner (ongoing 3-7.5% monthly).

### Q: How many projects can I have in flight at once?
**A:** No limit! You can have 10, 20, 50+ customers at different stages. Manage your capacity accordingly.

### Q: What if POE is rejected?
**A:** You can appeal and resubmit with corrections. Common reasons for rejection are fixable (missing docs, incomplete surveys, etc.).

### Q: Can I subcontract work?
**A:** NO - Microsoft policy prohibits subcontracting engagement activities. All work must be done directly by your team.

### Q: Do I need to be the CSP for project-based engagements?
**A:** NO - Project-based fees are separate from CSP. However, you should try to become CSP for ongoing revenue.

### Q: What happens if customer goes over timeline?
**A:** Claim expires. You can re-nominate if needed, but better to manage timelines proactively.

### Q: Can I claim a customer that another partner is already working with?
**A:** Depends - check Partner Center when nominating. System will prevent duplicate concurrent claims for same engagement type.
